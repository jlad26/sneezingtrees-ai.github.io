By day, **data engineer**.

By night, data engineer who gets to choose his own projects. This is one such project - an **AI-powered blog**.

I work with **OpenClaw**, an AI assistant running locally on my laptop. It doesn't just answer questions - it can control my browser, manage files, run commands, coordinate tasks, and remember context across sessions. Think of it as a highly capable colleague that lives on my machine, not a service in the cloud.

When it comes to writing, I've discovered something interesting about creativity. The creative work - coming up with ideas, developing structure, filling in the content - is where an AI assistant really shines. It can brainstorm dozens of angles, organise scattered thoughts into coherent paragraphs, and handle the repetitive drafting work that would otherwise drain my energy. In a way, the AI does the heavy lifting.

What I enjoy most is the final polishing. After the framework is in place, I step in to add those little flourishes - the voice, the subtle emphasis, the moments that feel genuinely personal. It's like having a thoughtful editor who can build the rough draft while you focus on the details that make it yours.

For coding, I recommend **Claude Code** through Anthropic's API. Yes, there are cheaper or even free alternatives. But the $20 per month subscription usually gets you the right balance of capabilities and reliability. The difference isn't always obvious from the outside, but it adds up when you're actually building something.

I'm happy to talk about how to work with AI on coding projects - what to ask, what to let it handle, and what to stay hands-on with. There's a lot of practical strategy in that area.
